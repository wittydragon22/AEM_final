Our project should be framed as a decision-support analysis, not just a collection of charts. The main research question should be:

Research question: How do crime incidents in Chicago vary by time, place, and crime type in 2024, and which district-time-crime combinations appear to need the most public safety attention?

The final notebook should answer this question by combining five parts: data collection and cleaning, temporal analysis, spatial analysis, crime type/arrest/COPA supplement analysis, and final synthesis. The final product now includes a simple priority table that ranks districts using crime volume, arrest rate, and station-distance context.

High-grade direction: the notebook should not feel like four disconnected mini-projects. The final integration should connect all sections to one decision question: where, when, and for which offense types should public safety attention be prioritized? The final table and presentation should return to this question.

5. Decision-Support Output

To make the project stronger, add a final ranked table. This is the most important remaining improvement because it turns the project from descriptive analysis into a Python-based decision-support system.

Component Example Calculation Meaning
Crime volume Count incidents by district, day_of_week, hour, and primary_type. Shows where and when incident concentration is high.
Arrest rate Mean of arrest for each group. Highlights areas or types where incidents are less often followed by arrest.
Station distance Average or median nearest-station distance for mapped incidents. Adds geographic context, but should not be treated as response-time evidence.
Priority score Standardize each component, then combine: high volume + low arrest rate + higher distance. Ranks combinations that may deserve attention or further investigation.

This table does not need to be mathematically advanced. A transparent formula and careful explanation are better than a complicated method nobody can explain during the presentation.

Recommended next notebook addition: create a final section named Decision-Support Priority Ranking. Group by district, day_of_week, hour, and primary_type, keep groups with enough records to be meaningful, then rank them by high incident count, low arrest rate, and higher nearest-station distance. This will directly answer the main research question.
